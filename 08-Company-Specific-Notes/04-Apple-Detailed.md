## **Phone Screen (Coding)**

**What Apple cares about:** Can you solve hard problems correctly?

### **Problem Style**

- Hard LeetCode (Apple-tagged)
- Examples: [LRU Cache](https://leetcode.com/problems/lru-cache/), [Number of Islands](https://leetcode.com/problems/number-of-islands/), [Binary Tree Max Path Sum](https://leetcode.com/problems/binary-tree-maximum-path-sum/), [Trapping Rain Water](https://leetcode.com/problems/trapping-rain-water/)

### **Evaluation Criteria (1â€“5 scale)**

- **5:** Optimal solution on first try; clean code; excellent communication
- **4:** Correct solution; minor optimization; good communication
- **3:** Correct with hints; acceptable communication
- **<3:** Incorrect or incomplete

### **Strategy**

1. **Clarify (2 min):** Constraints, edge cases. Apple likes precision.
2. **Think aloud (5 min):** Explain your approach; don't jump to code.
3. **Code (20â€“25 min):** Clean, readable, correct.
4. **Test (8 min):** Walkthroughs; edge cases; verify correctness.
5. **Optimize (5 min):** Discuss time/space; alternatives.

### **Real Interview Tips**

- Apple interviewers expect **correctness first**; optimization is secondary.
- They value **clean code**; they're thinking about code review standards.
- If you get stuck, ask questions; Apple is fair in its interviews.

### **Round 2: Coding or Design (60 min)**

Depending on your level:

- **ICT2â€“3:** Another coding round (hard problem)
- **ICT4+:** System design round

---

### **Round 3: System Design (60 min) [if not Round 2]**

**Typical prompts:**

- Design Siri (speech recognition + command execution)
- Design App Store
- Design iCloud backup system
- Design a recommendation engine for Apple Music
- Design security system for Apple ecosystem

**Apple's twist:** Emphasis on **privacy**, **security**, **reliability**, and **user experience**.

**Template:**

1. Clarify: Requirements, scale, privacy constraints.
2. High-level design: Components, data flow.
3. Deep dive: Pick one critical component (security, privacy, UX).
4. Security/Privacy: "How would we protect user data?"
5. Reliability: "What if services fail? How do we gracefully degrade?"

**Apple interviewer focus:**

- Do you think about privacy and security from day one?
- Can you design reliable systems?
- Do you consider user experience?

### **Round 4: Behavioral (45â€“60 min)**

**What Apple cares about:** Can you work on quality? Can you make decisions?

**Common questions:**

- "Tell me about a time you were really proud of your work."
- "Describe a project where you had to balance multiple constraints (speed, quality, cost)."
- "Tell me about a time you had to convince someone to do something differently."
- "Give an example of when you had to think deeply about a problem."
- "Tell me about a time you had to work with ambiguous requirements."

**STAR format:**

- **Situation:** 1â€“2 sentences
- **Task:** Challenge or decision (1â€“2 sentences)
- **Action:** What you did; emphasize rigor + quality + decision-making (4â€“5 sentences)
- **Result:** Outcome; what you learned (2â€“3 sentences)

**Apple-specific:**

- Emphasize **quality.** "We spent extra time to get it right."
- Emphasize **user impact.** "This improved user experience for millions."
- Emphasize **thoughtful decision-making.** "We considered X, Y, Z and chose Z because..."

---

### **Round 5: Technical Depth or Hiring Manager (60 min)**

Interviewer dives deep into your experience or your manager discusses fit.

**For technical depth:**

- Expect questions about systems you've built
- Be ready to discuss trade-offs
- Don't BS; admit what you don't know

**For hiring manager:**

- Discuss team, culture, growth opportunities
- Ask thoughtful questions
- Show genuine interest in the specific team

## **Interview Style**

Apple's interviews are:

- **Rigorous:** Expect high standards for code quality and design.
- **Fair:** No trick questions; interviewers are evaluating capability, not gotchas.
- **Curious:** Interviewers care about your problem-solving approach, not just the answer.
